FESS Setup: Why You Need the Toriumi Rhinoplasty Tray (Not the Septoplasty Tray)

One of the most common mistakes new surgical techs make on FESS cases: pulling the wrong tray. Here's why it matters and how to get it right every time.

The Mistake

You get told you're scrubbing a FESS with septoplasty. You think: "septoplasty tray." Wrong. For FESS, you need the Toriumi RHINOPLASTY tray — not the septoplasty tray. All your speculums come from the rhinoplasty tray.

What's in the Toriumi Tray

  • Freer elevator
  • Cottle elevator
  • Swivel knife, D knife
  • Jensen Middleton
  • Morselizer
  • ALL speculums — you get every speculum from this tray

💡 Pro Tip: Also make sure you're using ESS scopes — NOT Xomed scopes. They look similar but they're different systems. Your surgeon will notice.

Other Trays for FESS

  • ESS tray — Blakesley forceps, sinus seeker, tru-cut forceps
  • Giraffe forceps tray — 70° instruments, Kerrison, backbiter, delicate tip malleable suctions
  • Advanced frontal sinus tray — HOLD (standby only)

Your First Robotic Glossectomy: What Nobody Tells You About TORS Setup

Robotic surgery is intimidating the first time. Here's what the scrub tech needs to know that doesn't come in a manual.

It's NOT a Sterile Case

This surprises a lot of techs. A TORS glossectomy is not a sterile case in the traditional sense — you can use blue gloves to set up. Don't waste time with sterile technique during robot draping.

Know Your Surgeon's Order

This is critical — the entire case flow depends on which surgeon you're working with:

  • Dr. Kwon: Robot FIRST → then neck dissection 2nd. Needs extra drapes for the neck portion.
  • Dr. Sinha: Neck dissection FIRST — clips the lingual artery to prevent bleeding during robot portion. Then robot 2nd. No extra drapes needed.

💡 Pro Tip: Cut a 24Fr red Robinson catheter in half, connect to a Y connector, then to suction. This gives you 2 AP suctions on the field. Simple, smart setup.

Robot Draping — The Yellow Error

When draping the robot arms, if you get a yellow error on an arm drape — you messed up. Redo it. Magnets go on the RED sensors — the robot will start talking to you once they're on correctly.

Organ Procurement: The Ice Rule and 5 Other Things Nobody Teaches You

Multi-organ procurement is unlike any other case you'll scrub. Here's what to know before your first one.

Rule #1: ICE ICE ICE

Make 4 basins of ice MINIMUM. Fill those warmer machines with ice. Have extra bags ready. You think you have enough? Make more. Multiple transplant teams are counting on you to keep these organs viable.

The Legacy Boxes

You'll get 4 Legacy boxes. Each one has different stuff — sort them BEFORE the case:

  • One has Rumels
  • One has drapes
  • One has custom tubing
  • One has U-tapes

The Sternal Saw Blade

You need the Microaire sternal saw. But the blade? It's in the heart room. Send someone to get it BEFORE the case — not when the surgeon is asking for it.

💡 Pro Tip: Once cross-clamp happens, everything moves FAST. Have ALL your supplies ready before that moment. Every second of warm ischemia time = organ damage.

Tympanoplasty Instrument Guide: Rosen vs Barber, and the Gelfoam Trick

Ear cases have tiny instruments that all look the same. Here's how to tell them apart and prep the Gelfoam correctly.

Know Your Needles

  • Rosen needle = CURVED
  • Barber needle = STRAIGHT
  • Sickle knife = called "#1" — the surgeon will ask for "number one"

Pair all instruments — most come in pairs on the mayo. Bishop forceps, cup forceps (left and right), alligator forceps, Belluci scissors.

The Gelfoam Trick

You need 4 small Gelfoam packets (ask the nurse to get them). Mix with Ciprodex. Cut 2 packets into small strips (fourths), cut 2 packets into bigger strips (halves). Separate on tongue blades. Press soaked Gelfoam with Raytec to absorb excess. Roll into tiny rolls.

💡 Pro Tip: Color-code your medicine cups. RED = lidocaine with epi. WHITE = Ciprodex. YELLOW = Neo-Synephrine (for bleeders — cut cotton balls small, roll into balls, soak).

Fascia Drying

Place 2 packs of towels on the drill for the heat lamp. Put the harvested fascia on a block, place on top of towels to DRY. Start setting up Gelfoam on tongue blades once the surgeon asks for the dried fascia — not before.
